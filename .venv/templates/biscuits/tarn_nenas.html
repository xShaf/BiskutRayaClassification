Biscuit: Tart Nenas

Recipe

  • 250g butter
  • 3 tablespoon sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoon ghee
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla essence
  • 2 tablespoon powdered milk
  • 3 1/4 wheat flour
  • 2 tablespoon corn flour
  • 2 tablespoon custard flour
  • a little yellow food colouring
  • 2 egg yolk
  • 1/4 cup of fresh milk
  • 2 drops of lemon yellow food colouring(for egg wash)
  • pineapple jam(shaped into small balls)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the butter, sweetened condensed milk, vanilla essence, ghee, egg yolks, and yellow food coloring. Stir gently until everything is well mixed.
  2. Add the corn flour, custard powder, milk powder, and 1 cup of wheat flour. Mix until combined. Then, gradually add the remaining flour and knead until a smooth dough forms and doesn’t stick to your hands.
  3. Grease a baking tray lightly with butter. Using a tart mold, press out the dough. Place a ball of pineapple jam on top of the dough, roll it up carefully, and neaten the shape.
  4. For the egg wash, mix together the egg yolks, fresh milk, and lemon yellow food coloring. Stir until smooth. Brush the egg wash evenly over the rolled tarts.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 160°C (320°F) for about 25 minutes or until the tarts are cooked and slightly golden.
  6. Allow them to cool completely before storing in an airtight container.

History

Tart nenas became a cherished kuih raya in Malaysia as pineapples thrived in the local tropical climate, making the fruit both accessible and affordable. Over the decades, Malaysians began turning sweet pineapple jam into the star ingredient of these buttery, melt-in-the-mouth tarts. Today, pineapple tarts are deeply rooted in Malaysian festive culture, especially during Hari Raya, symbolizing warmth, hospitality, and the joy of sharing with family and friends.
