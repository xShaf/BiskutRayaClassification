Biscuit: Bahulu

Recipe

  • 5 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla essence
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (also known as wheat flour)

Instructions

  1. Add sugar and eggs, then beat until well combined. Add vanilla essence and continue beating for 5 minutes.
    (Beat until the mixture becomes fluffy and changes color for soft bahulu)
  2. Add the flour and beat for another 2 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 150°C for 10 minutes together with the mold inside.
  4. Remove the mold and grease it once with oil to prevent sticking.
  5. Pour the batter into the mold, filling about 3/4 full. Bake at 150°C for 12 minutes or adjust based on your oven.

History

According to the history of the olden days, the word "BAHULU" or "BAULU" is believed to have originated from the phrase "buah tangan dari hulu" (a gift from upstream). This is because people in the past primarily used waterways as their main mode of transportation. Due to the noble values of the Malay people, they held great respect for their neighbors and relatives. Bahulu was often given as a gift and became a medium to strengthen relationships among them.
