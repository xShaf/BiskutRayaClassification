Biscuit: Kacang Mazola

Recipe

  • 250g butter
  • 3 tbsp sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • 2 tbsp powdered milk
  • 3 1/4 cups wheat flour
  • 2 tbsp corn flour
  • 2 tbsp custard flour
  • A little yellow food coloring
  • 2 egg yolks (for egg wash)
  • 1/4 cup fresh milk
  • 2 drops yellow coloring (for egg wash)
  • Pineapple jam (shaped into balls)

Instructions

  1. Mix butter, condensed milk, ghee, egg yolk, and vanilla essence together until creamy.
  2. Combine all flours and food coloring, then gradually mix with the wet ingredients to form a soft dough.
  3. Take small pieces of dough, flatten slightly, insert pineapple jam, and shape into balls.
  4. Mix egg yolks, fresh milk, and food coloring for the egg wash. Brush the tops of each cookie.

History

Biskut Kacang Mazola became popular in Malaysia around the mid-20th century, inspired by local love for peanuts and the use of corn oil brands like Mazola, which gave the cookie its name. Traditionally made during festive seasons like Hari Raya, these peanut cookies became a staple on Malaysian kuih raya tables because of their rich, nutty flavor, crumbly texture, and simple, affordable ingredients. Over time, the recipe was shared and adapted among families, becoming a beloved classic passed down through generations.
